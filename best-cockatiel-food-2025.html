Cockatiels require specialized nutrition that differs significantly from other pet birds. Their natural diet in Australia consists of grass seeds, native fruits, and vegetation that provides specific nutrient ratios essential for their health and longevity.

Many commercial cockatiel foods fail to meet these nutritional needs, leading to deficiencies that manifest as poor feather quality, behavioral issues, and shortened lifespans. Key Nutritional Considerations

When choosing cockatiel food, prioritize pellets over seed mixes. High-quality pellets provide balanced nutrition that seed-only diets cannot match. Look for foods with 14-18% protein, 8-12% fat, and adequate vitamin A content.

Avoid foods with artificial colors, excessive sugar, or unknown preservatives. Your cockatiel's diet should consist of 75-80% pellets, 20-25% fresh vegetables and fruits, with seeds only as occasional treats.
